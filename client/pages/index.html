Explanation

> How does it work?

Every month, three brackets of 6 charts are selected for the score clash. The lower bracket is for less experienced players, up to 6th dan. The upper bracket is for more experienced players, 7th dan and above. The master bracket is aimed at players around 9th to 10th dan and up.

Each bracket has 5 normal charts and 1 boss chart. The boss chart is an extra challenge that yields 2x the points of a normal chart. It is indicated in bold yellow in the score and chart table. The boss chart is harder to score on, but highly recommended to try anyway, as it can significantly boost your score.

Your score is calculated based on your placement among other players on the same chart. The formula for this is as follows:

rating = 100 * (1 - ((placement - 1) / total players))

100 points is the maximum score you can achieve on a chart. 0 points is the minimum score you can achieve on a chart (for example, if you haven't achieved any score). After that, the weighted average of the ratings of all charts is calculated, with the number of players per chart as the weight. Again, the boss chart is weighted double.

How do I read the chart levels?

The difficulty scale in IIDX ranges from 1 to 12. IIDX has 5 different difficulty brackets: Beginner, Normal, Hyper, Another and Leggendaria. These are abbreviated as B, N, H, A and L respectively. There are also two different play modes: Single (7key+1) and Double (14key+2). These are abbreviated as SP and DP

Let's look at some examples:

  • SPA10. This stands for Single Play Another level 10.
  • DPN4, which stands for Double Play Normal level 4.
